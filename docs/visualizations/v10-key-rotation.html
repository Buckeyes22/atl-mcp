Caption (for the doc)

The audit-signing keypair rotates without breaking the chain because every entry pins both its key_id (sha256 of the public key) and the git_ref of the registry version it was signed under. A verifier resolves at-the-time-of-signing, not at-time-of-verification — so old entries remain verifiable forever, even after rotation. Two flavors of rotation: routine (scheduled, on a cadence) and incident-driven (runbook §C — revoke flag set, post-window entries reviewed).
